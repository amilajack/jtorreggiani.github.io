About Me

I'm a human on a mission to do the most good I can do.

Over 9 years ago, as an undergraduate majoring in history, I was inspired by the struggles and triumphs of the human species. Dismayed by the suffering and environmental degradation I was seeing in the world, I became dedicated to making a positive difference any way I could.

I realized that science presented potential solutions to many of the challenges facing society. After graduating in 2010, I began a period of intensive self-directed learning and further education in STEM. I developed an affinity for computing and have been building a successful career in the software industry ever since.

Today, I am a senior software engineer at Artemis, a market leading enterprise Cultivation Management Platform (CMP). In addition to my professional work, I contribute to philanthropic efforts including mentoring, volunteering, donating, and conducting research.

Highlights

  • Lead development of a mobile application portal for a virtual agronomy platform. Managed the project from requirements gathering to deployment and maintenance.
  • Integrated digital course material management service provider's billing process with leading enterprise student information system via REST API. Automated manual reconciliation processes for university accounts receivable departments.
  • Developed an automated call-centre service for a Fortune 500 pharmaceutical company's product release. Delivered solution two months ahead of schedule and $15K under budget.
  • Invited by the College of Saint Rose to present research on the use of crowdsourcing to address the problems of corporate greed and income inequality.
  • Guest lecturer on environmental physics and sustainable technologies at the State University of New York at Albany.
